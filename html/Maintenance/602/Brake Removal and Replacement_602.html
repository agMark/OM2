Brake details are shown in Fig Cross Reference. For instructions concerning brake maintenance and overhaul, refer also to Cleveland (Parker) Document AWBCMM0001-22.

Brake fluid used is red Mil-H-5606A petroleum-base fluid, which is used in most light aircraft. Do not use automotive brake fluid as this will swell the o-rings.

The wheel units can be removed by using a wrench to remove the six bolts attaching the two halves of each brake assembly. The brake line may remain connected if only the linings are to be changed. Linings are Cleveland p/n 066-06600 and are installed over dowel pins. There are 24 linings per aircraft.

Each brake assembly has three Cleveland p/n 092-05501 pistons. The o-ring installed in the cylinder is Cleveland p/n MS28775-128.

To change piston o-rings, remove the brake line, install a 1/4-28 bolt in the piston and pull it from the caliper housing. The pistons are aluminum and should be checked for wear or burrs that could cut the o-ring. Check the bore in the housing at the same time. When installing new o-rings, soak them in MIL-H-5606 brake fluid before replacement. Reinstall the pistons with care so that the o-ring is not damaged.
