The elevators are attached to the stabilizers and to the pedestal in the fuselage with AN4-10A bolts, with an AN960-416 washer under the bolt head and two washers under the AN365-428A nut (except at the pedestal where only one washer is under the nut). The elevator horns connect to the aft pushrod with an AN4-10A bolt, two AN960-416 washers (placed to give maximum fuselage clearance) and an AN365-428A nut.

Like the rudder, the elevators and center pedestal have NMB p/n MS14104-4 spherical bearings installed. Due to bearing drag, it is very important for all bolts connecting the elevators and the horns to be fully torqued (100 inch-pounds at the nut). We have seen elevator horns with badly elongated holes because elevators had been replaced in the field and bolts were not fully torqued.

Elevator stops are located at the elevator horn. The down stop uses p/n 70067-1 neoprene washers under compression to absorb shocks. Because this washer may occasionally split and fall out, verify that the washer is in place during 100-hour inspections. Also, if the control stick is pulled back sharply against the up-stop, or if a severe wind gust deflects the elevator up, the up-stop bracket may bend and may be re-straightened as necessary.

Check the elevator travel to make sure it is within limits specified in the Type Certificate Data Sheet, provided below for convenience:

  • Up Limit: 29.0° ± 1.0°
  • Down Limit: 16.0° ± 1.0°
