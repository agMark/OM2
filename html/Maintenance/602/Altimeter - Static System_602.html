The static ports on the AT-602 are located in the aft section of the fuselage. The static line is accessible beneath the right-hand fuselage skin that attaches to the top of the hopper tank.

Is this static line access correct for the 602?

CAUTION

Apply air pressure only to the line that leads to the pitot-static head. Do not apply air pressure to the line that leads to the instrument panel. Sensitive instruments may be damaged.

The static ports on the AT-602 are the low points of the system and are normally self-draining, but there are plugged “T" fittings on the rear face of the static port buttons in the aft fuselage. These plugs may be removed to drain residual moisture in the static system lines.

Pitot/Static Calibration

Using a pitot static tester, attach the positive pressure line to the pitot tube and negative pressure line to the static port. The pitot tube is located under the left-hand wing. To maintain compliance with FAR Part 91.411, refer to FAR Part 43 Appendix E, which outlines the procedures for performing the altimeter systems test and transponder inspection.
