Details of the tailwheel fork are shown in Fig Cross Reference. The tailwheel fork may be removed from the housing by removing the single AN5C32A stainless bolt from the arm assembly connected to the centering springs. With the fork removed, check for wear on the spindle at the bronze bearing location. Also check for condition of the steel bushing that the lock pin drops into. This bushing is pressed into the steel plate at the bottom end of the spindle and staked with a center punch in four places. This holds the p/n 40042-1 bushing in place since it has a beveled edge on both sides. Excess wear on the fork spindle at the bronze bearing location is an indication that grease is not reaching the needed areas. To properly grease the bronze bearing, jack the tailwheel clear of the ground and apply MIL-G-81322 grease at the fitting on the housing. Rotate the fork until grease is running out of the full perimeter of both upper and lower bearings. Excessive wear in the spindle requires a new p/n 40117-1 fork assembly (which includes the steel lock pin bushing). The centering springs are p/n 40044-1 and attach to a p/n 40035-1 bracket that wears on the p/n 40036-1 bushing. The bolt is an AN4C7A.
