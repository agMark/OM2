The wing is of all-metal construction, with the main spar designed to take the entire bending load. Thick wing skins are employed near the wing root with a thickness reduction at each skin lap as wing loads diminish. No spanwise stringers are used to simplify construction and repairs and because wrinkles in the upper skin and canning between the ribs will be noticed during maneuvers with heavy loads. This is a natural condition and, unless the canning or wrinkles change noticeably in a particular location, there is no cause for alarm. Any changes as mentioned would require close inspection of all internal structural parts in the area where the change has taken place.

The wing leading edge is .040 thick 2024-T3 and may be replaced. Do not remove more than one skin at a time or it is likely that the wing twist (washout) will be compromised. The wing rear spar attach plate is 5/16" thick 4130N steel for extra strength and for corrosion resistance to fertilizer exposure. It is cadmium plated and primed with Chromanoxide epoxy primer before assembly to the rear spar.

All wing parts are cleaned with a detergent soap, scrubbed with an etching solution, dipped into an Alodine 1200 solution and then primed before assembly. Skin laps on the top side of the wing are sealed with fuel tank sealant to prevent chemical entry, similarly to that performed at the wing root.
