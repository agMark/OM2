Gap Covers

The gap covers are attached with 90008-1 Phillips truss head #4 x 1/4" long stainless self-tapping screws. A No. 39 hole is drilled for the screw installation. The Cessna S202VW rubber channel is attached with 3-M 8001 Adhesive.

Trim Controls

There is a p/n 30137-1 (left-hand) and 30137-2 (right-hand) trim control idler attached on the respective inboard end of each stabilizer rear spar. Each trim idler has two Bunting P26-10 bushings press fitted inside the welded assembly with a p/n 30070-1 spacer bushing allowing the AN3-32A attach bolt to be fully torqued (60 inch-pounds at the nut). The brackets that attach the trim idlers are p/n 30049-1 and 30049-2 and should never need replacing unless the AN3-32A bolt becomes loose and causes wear in the hole.

Two of the idler arms are attached to the pushrods connected to the trim tab horns. These arms have a Bunting P19-1 bushing press-fit into the idler arms. Both should be replaced if excessive trim tab free-play develops. The Bunting bushings are impregnated with oil, but if new ones are installed, fill the bushings to overflowing with general purpose oil and squeeze on the ends with finger pressure to force the oil into the small cavities inside of the bushings. The bushing connections should be oiled regularly.

Abrasion Tape

3M 8671-3 anti-abrasion tape is installed on the leading edge of the stabilizer. This tape should be periodically checked for condition and replaced as required. It is permissible to operate without the tape but expect paint life to be decreased.
