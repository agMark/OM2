The wiring schematic for the stall warning horn is shown in Fig Cross Reference below. The horn is connected in series with the lift-detector switch that is located near midspan of the left-hand wing leading edge.

The lift-detector switch is controlled by the position of a tab extending forward from the wing leading edge. The weight of the tab holds it down during lower angles of attack, allowing the switch to remain open. As the angle of attack increases, airflow eventually causes the tab to pivot upward, closing the switch and causing the warning horn to sound.
