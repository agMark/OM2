The stabilizer struts attach to the fuselage frame with an AN6C13A bolt and to the stabilizer eyebolts with AN6-11A bolts. There are two eyebolts that connect to a welded steel brace inside the stabilizer for strut attachment.

The struts are constructed of 2.36 x 1.00 x .049 4130N streamlined tubing (which may be repaired if necessary). Contact the factory for repair instructions since an internal tube is installed. The struts are oiled internally through the threaded fittings on the ends, and should be re-oiled if repaired. They are sandblasted and given a baked powdered coating for abrasion resistance. At various intervals, the struts should be re-sandblasted and finished as described above. Be sure to remove the terminals and plug the open holes before sandblasting.

The stabilizer strut leading edges are typically protected with 3M 6671-3 anti-abrasion tape. Thicker protection tape or material should not be installed as damage can occur (ref SL409). The tape should be periodically inspected for condition and replaced as required.
