To rig the stabilizers, the elevators must be removed (See ). The factory practice is to adjust the terminal in the forward strut so that approximately the same amount of threads is showing on both right-hand and left-hand struts. Pin he struts to the fuselage fittings and the stabilizers to the fuselage attach fittings (don't forget the washers under each bolt head) but do not install the nuts. Have someone hold up each stabilizer tip and connect the strut to each rear eyebolt only. Sight down the elevator hinge line to see that the hinges on both sides of the aircraft line up within .03". Adjust each strut length the same amount until both hinges are in line. Stand directly behind the aircraft exactly on the fuselage centerline and raise or lower your line of sight until the same point of each stabilizer falls in the line of sight with a reference point on the wings. It is likely that the terminal on one strut will have to be lengthened a certain number of turns and the opposite strut shortened the same number of turns so that the stabilizers are perpendicular to the vertical axis of the aircraft.

The factory procedure is to align the face of the eyebolts with the strut clevis before the nut on the eyebolt is fully torqued. There is a plug button installed in the skin above the nut.

Adjust the terminal at the front eyebolt on each side so that the bolts may be inserted without causing a twist in the stabilizers. With all bolts now in place, it is necessary to recheck the hinge side-to-side alignment and stabilizer position with the wings. If all is still in order, install the washers and nuts on all attach bolts (five each side). Torque the fuselage attachment bolts to 200 inch-pounds at the nut and torque the AN6 (3/8") bolts to 300 inch-pounds at the nut. Tighten the check nuts of each terminal to 290 inch-pounds torque. Be sure the terminal is threaded into the strut fitting far enough by inserting a piece of safety wire in the small inspection hole at each terminal location.

Recheck nut installation on the stabilizer-to-frame location and use 90008-1 screws to install the gap covers. If the rubber channel is loose on the gap cover, reattach with 3-M 8001 adhesive before the gap cover is installed.
