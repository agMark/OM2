Details of the wing front-spar and rear-spar attachments to the fuselage are shown below in Fig Cross Reference and Fig Cross Reference.

Two aluminum and two steel wing-attach angles are used to attach the wing main spar to the fuselage vertical tube just aft of the main spar. There are four AN5-26A bolts that attach each vertical tube to the wing angles.

It should also be noted that there is no bolt through the attach angle and small leg of the lower spar cap. This is to eliminate bolt fretting and make the joint more fatigue resistant.

The wing attach angles are p/n 20693-1. They are attached to the main spar with six AN5-21A and two AN5-14A bolts. If it is necessary to change the wing attach angles due to bolt wear or fuselage repairs, ask the factory at the time they are ordered for detailed instructions on how to drill the attach angles since these angles are drilled to match each individual aircraft.

The rear spar plate is attached to the fuselage frame with a single NAS1309-23 bolt. These bolts wear slightly and should be checked at 1,000 hour intervals and replaced if worn or corroded. Do not use AN bolts at this location. To remove the bolts, first remove the lower wing gap cover screws. Removal detail of the gap cover is shown below in Fig Cross Reference.

Any time an Air Tractor plane suffers damage to the leading edge of a wing, the associated rear spar attaching structure needs to be carefully inspected for damage. The rear spar connecting bolt should be replaced and the wing rear spar plate connection to the rear spar should also be carefully inspected. Cracks in the paint or the sealer where the plate attaches to the rear spar are indications of possible damage.

If your plane has suffered wing damage from striking an obstacle (such as a pole or a wire) or incurred other accident damage, carefully inspect the wing rear spar attaching structure for damage. Failure to detect and repair damage to the rear spar attaching structure potentially caused by other wing damage may subsequently result in wing separation from the plane while in flight.

Place a small hydraulic jack under the wing rear spar plate and apply only a small amount of pressure to support the wing when the bolt is removed. Lock the parking brake so the aircraft will not move. Remove the nut and gently drive the bolt out with a small hammer, protecting the threads from damage. Visually inspect the bolt and then place it on a smooth plate, rotating it check for bending. Inspect for thread damage or rust. Do not use excessive force to remove the bolts or apply heat. If there is difficulty in removing the bolts, contact the factory.

We encourage replacement of old bolts with new bolts at all times, but if the old bolts are in good shape and time intervals mentioned above are not exceeded, they may be reinstalled. Make sure to grease the bolts with MIL-G-81322 grease before installing. Torque values are 540 inch-pounds (torque wrench on nut).
