The fuselage frame is made of 4130N tubing. It may be repaired in conventional ways. AC-43.13 may be used as a guide for repairs to tubing. All fittings are also of 4130N material. The repaired area should be sanded and burned paint should be removed to a point where it can be feather-edged by sanding. A good etching solution would be a mixture of 1/2 fluid oz. of concentrated phosphoric acid in one gallon of isopropanol (isopropyl alcohol). If relative humidity is extremely low, add 1/4 cup of distilled water to the solution. Factory finish is either R9006 Air Ag Yellow Epoxy with R3203 converter or gray epoxy primer (Contact Air Tractor Customer Service for specific materials). See for complete repainting instructions.

If your aircraft does not already have an overturn skid plate installed, it is highly recommended to install one using the information located in Service Letter #97.

Reported Issues

For serial numbers 602-0337 thru 602-0664, there have been reports of cracks or failures of the upper longeron on the left hand side of the fuselage frame just forward of the stabilizer front spar attach fitting. We believe that these cracks or failures are associated with high vertical loads during repeated hard turns. For more information regarding inspections and repairs, refer to Service Letter #195.
