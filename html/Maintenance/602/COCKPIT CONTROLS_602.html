The control stick assembly is p/n 70346-1 and is attached to the 70050-7 torque tube assembly with a NAS1304-44 bolt. The lower end of the control stick (p/n 70043-1) is machined aluminum and houses two Fafnir KP4 bearings held in place with two p/n 1300-93 snap rings. The control stick weld assembly is p/n 70047-1, is chrome plated and internally oiled. The control stick grip is Automatic Flagman p/n NA1-135.

A p/n 70294-2 elevator pushrod connects the control stick to the forward idler assembly with AN4-13A bolts in both ends. The pushrod has a Fafnir RE4H6 bearing in each end, allowing length adjustment.

The 70050-7 control stick torque tube is supported at each end with p/n 70160-1 housing assembly, attaching to the fuselage frame with AN5-14A bolts. Each housing assembly has a grease fitting installed and the grease should be applied during 12-month inspections at a minimum. The bronze bearings in the housings are Bunting EF1416-16 and may be replaced as required. After a period of time this flanged bushing will wear, causing fore-and-aft slack in the torque tube as the elevators are moved up and down by the control stick. The slack may be removed by adding a 7/8" ID x 1-3/8" OD .010" thick shim (p/n 3088A325). With the torque tube all the way against the rear flange, clearance on the front flange should be .001-.005". If the shim is a little too thick, the face of the forward flange may be faced off slightly.

The forward end of the torque tube has p/n 70149-1 boot and 70144-1 boot ring installed with 90008-1 screws attaching the boot ring to the floor. On the aft cockpit wall, p/n 70148-1 boot and 70142-1 ring seal the forward elevator pushrod entrance. The hopper handle uses p/n 80168-1 boot and 70140-1 boot ring, the spray lever pushrod uses p/n 80131-1 boot and 70154-1 boot ring and the trim pushrod uses p/n 70070-1 boot and 70143-1 boot ring.

A p/n 70478-1 control lock assembly is installed with two AN42B-4A eyebolts connected to the fuselage frame. An Ajax No. 38 spring keeps the control lock retracted when not in use.
