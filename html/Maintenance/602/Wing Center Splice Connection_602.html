Details of the wing center-splice connection are shown below in Fig Cross Reference.

The main spar caps of each wing are attached to each other with with p/n 20602-1/-2 attach blocks (upper spar caps) and 21076-1/-2 attach blocks (lower caps). The main spar webs are attached to each other by p/n 20996-3 plate of .59" thick heat treated 4130 steel. The connection to the fuselage on the aircraft centerline is made with a p/n 20178-2 steel plate of .125 4130N connected to the fuselage structure with two AN6-23A bolts, and to the two 3/4" bolts through the 20602-1 upper spar cap attach block. There are four p/n 20606-1 tube-nut welded assemblies and two p/n 20608-1 sleeves in each wing installation. The sleeves and tube-nuts are drilled 23/64" and reamed .374 after the tube-nuts have been fully tightened, and two AN6-21A bolts installed in each sleeve. If the wings are removed from the aircraft, it is usually necessary to replace the four tube-nut assemblies and the two sleeves since the .374 reamed holes through both parts will probably not line up. In this case, tighten the new tube-nuts 1920 inch-pounds torque, position the new sleeves mid-way between the tube nuts, drill 23/64" through the sleeve and tube-nut, and ream .374 for the AN6-21A bolt installation.

The current 602OM always references reaming to .375" instead of the 802 values of .374". I have left this dimension since it is slightly more conservative. Part numbers copied from current 602OM.

The tube-nut and sleeve installation is to take the “kick-load" imposed by dihedral angle and cancel it out by transferring this load from the upper spar caps to the lower spar caps.

Torque values should be checked on the nuts to learn if the bolts have elongated or if fretting has loosened the fit of the joint. Torque values to be checked are as follows:

There are 40 NAS1306 bolts through the small legs of the spar caps that attach to the 20996-3 plate on the aft side of the web. There are 18 NAS1306-18 bolts through the spar web that attach the 20996-3 plate. These 3/8" nuts are checked for torque at 300 inch-pounds. There are 14 NAS1204-21 screws with AN364-428 nuts torqued to 80 inch-pounds. There are 26 NAS1304 bolts through the spar caps and 14 NAS1304-18 through the spar web that attach the 20996-3 plate with AN365-428 nuts torqued to 100 inch-pounds.

2000 Hour Center Splice Inspection

This procedure was originally published as Service Letter #411 which modified earlier guidance provided in this manual.

  • Remove inspection covers and side skins to gain access to the wing center splice area.
  • With a mirror, borescope, or other appropriate tool, visually inspect the center splice bolts. Specifically, look for signs of corrosion, bolt damage or looseness, fretting or any other signs that the center splice may require more in-depth inspection.
  • Inspect the entire splice connection carefully for signs of bolt working, corrosion, or cracks. The critical area for the lower spar cap is at station 9.2" from the aircraft center-line where the 3/8" NAS bolt attaches the 21076-1/-2 blocks to the main leg of the cap. If a crack were to develop at that point it would probably progress to the small leg of the cap at station 10.5". The next critical area of the lower spar cap would be in the rivet pattern attaching the small leg to the web from approximate stations 25.0" to 30.0" which is where the inboard fuel tank wall is located. It would also be advisable to check carefully the lower side of the main leg in the area where the fuel tank bottom ends.
  • If issues are noted that cannot be remedied (corrosion removal and touch up) through the access holes or side skins, remove the hopper for further inspection and repair.
