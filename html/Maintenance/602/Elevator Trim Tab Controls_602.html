Details of the elevator trim tab controls are shown in Cross Reference Placeholder. The elevator trim tab system uses the elevator tabs as both trim tabs and elevator boost tabs. The boost tabs function to relieve a portion of the control load during maneuvers.

Elevator trim is controlled by a handwheel on the left-hand wall of the cockpit. The handwheel is shaft-connected to a two-sheave drum which drives two taut and opposing cables. The cables are to be tensioned to 10-15 lb. At their opposite end, these are connected to a pulley shaft positioning a worm gear in a gearbox located in the aft fuselage. The worm-gear box is shown below in Fig Cross Reference.

The worm gear drives a sector gear with an exposed sector arm attached on the aft side of the gatebox. The sector arm is connected by a pushrod to a bellcrank transmitting the motion to an idler just forward of the horizontal stabilizer aft edge. One of these idlers is shown below in Fig Cross Reference.

A p/n 70314-1 trim bellcrank is attached to the fuselage frame with AN3-14A bolts, Bunting P26-8 bushings, p/n 70065-1 spacer bushings, AN365-1032 nuts and AN960-10 washers under the nuts. The bellcrank should be removed during annual inspections and bushings inspected for wear and lubricated.

The adjustable pushrods from the trim bellcrank to the idler arm on the stabilizer rear spar are p/n 70153-1. The ends are AN486-4 clevis ends and AN316-4 check nuts. Attachments are with AN3-6 bolts, AN310-3 nuts, AN960-10 washers and MS24665-151 cotter pins. The pushrod going forward from the bellcrank is p/n 70498-1 with a Fafnir REB3N bearing, and is connected to the bellcrank with a AN3-7A bolt, AN960-10 washer under the bolt head, AN970-3 washer under the nut and a AN365-1032 nut.

Fairleads are p/n 70122-1 with 40081-00 snap rings. The forward end going into the cockpit connects to the top hole in the trim lever with the same hardware used connecting to the trim bellcrank.

Paragraph copied from current 602OM.

Two p/n 70559-1 pushrods attach to the trim tab horn with AN3-7A bolts and AN365-1032 nuts; and to the idler arm in the stabilizer with AN3-6 bolts, AN310-3 nuts, AN960-10 washers and MS24665-151 cotter pins. Be sure the pushrod is positioned with the bend on the forward end approximately under the elevator spar tube. There is a Bunting P19-1 bushing at the forward joint which should be replaced if the tab free play is excessive. If there is still excessive free play check the bolts for wear and pushrod ends for wear. Install new pushrods if the holes are worn.
