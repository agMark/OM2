Details of the spray plumbing are shown below in Fig Cross Reference.

The plumbing from the hopper gate box to the spray pump consists of a p/n 80972-1 tube assembly and 80376-3 hose attached to the 80972-1 tube assembly. The hose is clamped with QS200M56S stainless steel clamps.

The plumbing from the discharge side of the pump to the valve is p/n 80657-5 and 80657-6 and has a p/n 80768-1 coupler at the valve end. The hoses are p/n 80399-1 and are double clamped with QS200M48S clamps. In addition, a p/n 80774-1 strap is used to safety the tube to the top of the pump to prevent the 80657-5 tube from water hammer effects and blowing off due to high pressure.

The tube attached to the strainer is p/n 80766-1 assembly (2 ea.). The straps on the support assemblies are p/n 80383-1 and are attached with AN4C6A bolts. D4-13T-303 Faspins are used to attach the support assemblies to the fuselage frame.

In some operations, high-volume spray work and/or rapid spray valve operation can cause hydraulic shock in the spray system. This shock, known as “water hammer”, can cause a pressure spike that can damage the pressure sensor. A small, inexpensive orifice may be installed to help isolate the pressure sensor from this pressure spike, preventing the sensor from being damaged. This orifice may be retrofitted to your aircraft using the instructions found in Service Letter #308.
