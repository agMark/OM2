Verify that the flaps are rigged.

Set the control lock and adjust the two 70554-1 idlers to lean inboard 3-1/2°, which positions the idler 90° to the aileron push-tube inside the wing.

The ailerons are rigged with the trailing edge 1/8"-3/16" below the flap trailing edge so that flight loads will bring the trailing edge up approximately even with the flap trailing edge. Aileron travel (with flaps up) is 19° up (+/- 1°) and 14° down (+/- 1°). Aileron control stops are located under the cockpit floor on each side of the control stick torque tube horn. Additional up-aileron stops are located in the wing at the aileron bellcrank. The adjustment consists of tightening or loosening the bolts that compress a stack of 4 to 6 neoprene washers (p/n 70067-1). The neoprene washers act as shock absorbers for the control system, but sometimes split and fall out. It is advisable to inspect the stops and replace the neoprene washers as necessary during 12-month inspections.

There is an aileron stop bracket attached to the wing rib. It is designed so that the aileron bellcrank contacts a spring-loaded plunger, and then as the aileron moves farther, a hard stop establishes the final limit of aileron travel.

There has been one incident recorded of a parked aircraft experiencing a hard windstorm from the rear, causing deflection of the stop bracket sufficiently that the plunger slipped past the bellcrank arm and would not return. If this occurs, the ailerons will be frozen in a fully deflected position.

To prevent this from happening in flight, Air Tractor redesigned the bracket holding the spring plunger to be more rigid and eliminate the possibility that the plunger will slip past the arm. Operators should verify the bellcrank is solidly striking the plunger.
