The ailerons are all-metal and require very little maintenance. The lead counterweight may sometimes loosen on the long AN3-35A bolt through the lead and support tube. If the counterweight is loose, remove the counterweight and support tube, drilling and reaming for a larger diameter bolt. To do this, remove the aileron from the aircraft by removing the nuts from three hinge fittings, remove four screws in the inspection plate where the pushrod attaches to the wing bellcrank and remove the nut from the bolt passing through the bellcrank and pushrod. Note that there is a spacer washer between the aluminum hinge and support arm bearing, and a AN970-4 large washer on the outboard side of the bearing under the bolt head.

With the AN4-10A bolts pointing inboard, the correct order of parts would be a AN970-4 washer under the bolt head, AN960-416L washer, bearing, AN960-416 washer, aluminum aileron hinge, AN960-416 washer and AN365-428 nut. The steel inboard hinge uses a AN4-25A bolt with an AN960-416 washer under the head, AN970-4 washer, bearing, steel hinge, AN960-416 washer and AN365-428 nut. Leave the pushrod attached to the aileron and reach through the inspection plate opening to remove the bolt attaching the pushrod to the wing bellcrank. Verify that the control stick lock is still engaged and that the trailing edge of the aileron is well supported. Remove the three hinge bolts (center one first) while helpers support the weight of the aileron at each end. Lift up on the pushrod to prevent dragging and remove the aileron from the aircraft, placing it on a soft and stable surface. The counterweight may be removed from the aileron by removing the two plug buttons on the upper and lower sides of the aileron nose skin and the AN3-12A bolt that attaches the support tube to the support structure riveted to the aileron. The counterweight and support tube should be taken to the workbench where the long AN3-35A bolt may be removed. Leaving the parts assembled, clamp the counterweight to the base of the drill press. Use a long 3/16" drill bit in a drill press to align the spindle with the 3/16" hole that goes completely through the counterweight and support tube. Change to a 7/32" drill bit and drill through both parts, enlarging the existing 3/16" hole. Finally, use a .248" reamer to create the final and enlarged hole through both parts. Grease the new AN4-35A bolt and install.

Reinstall the counterweight and support tube on the aileron and check for a snug fit. In addition to the AN4-35A bolt used, apply PR-1422 A-2 during installation to help bond the counterweight assembly to the aileron. The AN3 bolts have a torque of 100 inch-pounds when applied to the head. Reinstall the aileron in the reverse order of removal, taking care that the washer arrangement is correct. The fastener passing through the pushrod and wing bellcrank is a AN4-12A bolt, using AN960-416 washers under both the bolthead and AN365-428A nut.

While the aileron is removed, check the aluminum hinges for secure attachment to the spar and the steel hinge for signs of corrosion, loose rivets or cracks in the welded areas. Inspect both ends of the pushrods for cracks in the threads, check the rod-end bearing (Fafnir RE4F6-2) for condition and lubrication, confirming the check nut against the bearing is snug.

After the aileron has been reinstalled and inspection of all connections has been made, reach through the inspection plate opening and check to see if the pushrod is free to rotate slightly as the aileron is moved through the full range of travel. After verifying the bolt through the pushrod and wing bellcrank has been torqued, reinstall the inspection plate with four screws.

The bearings in the aileron support arms are Fafnir KP4A, which are staked on both sides and press-fit into the 1/4" aluminum arm. These bearings are sealed, require no lubrication and should provide long life.
