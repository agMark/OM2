Attachment Bolts

Until 1997, the horizontal stabilizers were attached to the fuselage frame with two AN5-7A bolts.

Since, the horizontal stabilizers are attached with NAS6605 bolts, MS20002-5 washers and ESNA F52N1610-054 nuts as shown in and . Torque is 17 ft-lb (204 in-lb) on the nut.

On 12-month inspections the gap cover should be removed from the stabilizer and the bolt condition and torque checked. At the same time inspect the fuselage fittings closely for cracks.

PROPOSED--"Reports have been received of large birds striking the outboard end of the horizontal tail on various aircraft. In a few cases, the bird impacted near the gap between the elevator horn and the horizontal stabilizer. The damaged surface was pushed into the gap and caused the elevator horn to become obstructed. To help prevent the likelihood of jamming, Air Tractor is installing perforated ribs in the elevators and stabilizers of new aircraft.

Air Tractor recommends that all operators, especially those who operate in areas where impact with large birds are likely to have their aircraft modified to perforate the ribs of this area. The perforated ribs will allow the unsupported tip of the nose rib to break free if it becomes damaged and reduce the risk of binding. This change will also remove two non-structural closeout plates on those aircraft that have them installed. Refer to Service Letter #319 for additional information."--PROPOSED to reference SL #319.

Gap Covers

The gap covers are attached with stainless self-tapping Phillips truss head screws. A No. 39 hole is drilled for the screw installation. The Cessna rubber channel is attached with 3-M Adhesive.

Trim Controls

There are a left hand and right hand trim control idler attached to each stabilizer rear spar on the inboard end. Each trim idler has two Bunting bushings that are press fit inside the welded assembly with a spacer bushing, allowing the AN attach bolt to be fully torqued (60 inch-pounds at the nut). The brackets that attach the trim idlers shouldn't ever need replacing unless the AN bolt becomes loose and causes wear in the holes.

One of the idler arms is attached to the pushrod connected to the trim tab horn and this arm has a Bunting bushing press-fit into the idler arm. This is the same size bushing in the trim tab horn and should be replaced when excessive trim tab free play develops. The Bunting bushing is impregnated with oil. If a new one is installed, fill the bushing to overflowing with general purpose oil and apply finger pressure on the ends to force the oil into the small cavities inside the bushing . The bushing connections should be oiled regularly.

Abrasion Tape

3M anti-abrasion tape is installed on the leading edge of the stabilizer. This tape shouldbe periodically checked for condition and replaced as required. It is permissible to operate without the tape but expect paint quality and life to diminish.
