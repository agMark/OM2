The control stick assembly is attached to the torque tube assembly with a NAS bolt. The lower end of the control stick is a machined aluminum part which houses two Fafnir bearings held in place by two snap rings. The control stick weld assembly is chrome plated and oiled internally. The control stick grip is manufactured by Automatic Flagman.

A pushrod connects the control stick to the forward idler assembly with an AN bolt in each end. The pushrod has Fafnir bearings in each end, which allows length adjustment.

The torque tube is supported on each end with a housing assembly which attaches to the fuselage frame with AN bolts and a spacer block between the housing and the floor. Each housing assembly has a grease fitting installed and the grease should be applied at least during annual inspections. The bronze bushings in the housings are made by Bunting and may be replaced if required. The bushing is a flange type and after a period of time bushing wear will cause fore-and-aft slack in the torque tube as the elevators are moved up and down with the control stick. The slack may be removed by adding a .010 thick shim (7/8 I.D. x 1-3/8 O.D.). With the torque tube all the way against the rear flange, clearance on the front flange should be between .001 and .005. If the shim is a little too thick, the face of the forward flange may be faced off slightly if neccessary.

The forward end of the torque tube has a boot and boot ring installed, with screws attaching the boot ring to the floor. On the aft cockpit wall, a boot and boot ring seal the forward elevator pushrod entrance. The hopper handle, spray lever pushrod and trim pushrod also all use boots and boot rings.

A control lock assembly is attached to two AN eyebolts which are part of the instrument panel attachment. A spring keeps the control lock retracted when not in use.
