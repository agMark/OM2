The tail wheel is manufactured by Cleveland. Details of the Cleveland tail wheel are shown in Fig Cross Reference.

Tail wheel bearings are a 2-piece Timken cone (the part with the rollers) and cup (the part pressed into the wheel half). Hand pack the bearings with grease (MIL-G-81322) and install the wheel on the axle. Install the jam nut and tongue washer on the axle with the round shoulder inboard and tighten until all bearing slack is removed (but while the wheel is still free to rotate). Inflate the tire to 50 psi. Grease the axle bolt and install the wheel and axle assembly on the fork. Torque the nut to 160 inch-pounds. Safety the jam nut against the rotation of the wheel with Mil Spec stainless safety wire.

Keeping the bearings properly lubricated is the key to long bearing life.

To remove the tail wheel, jack the wheel clear of the ground per Section . and remove the long AN bolt that attaches the axle to the fork. Note the method of safety wire on the large stainless jam nut and cut the wire loose from the fork. The tail wheel is now free and ready for disassembly. Back off the jam nut from the axle and remove the axle. Inspect the axle for wear at the bearing positions, as insufficient greasing procedures will cause the bearing to turn on the axle and ruin both parts. Inspect the AN bolt for wear and straightness, and slide the bolt through the fork to check the hole in the fork for wear. There should be no wear in the fork holes since there is no movement between fork and bolt. If the tire is to be removed, deflat the tire first and then remove the bolts holding the wheel halves together.

Before reassembly, clean all parts. Partially inflate the tube to keep from pinching it during installation. Reassembly is opposite of disassembly.
