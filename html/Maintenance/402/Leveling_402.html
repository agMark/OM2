The airplane is leveled to “level flight attitude" by jacking the tailwheel. Refer to for proper jacking procedures. The airplane should be in this level position for aircraft weighing and other maintenance/ installation items that require the aircraft to be in level flight attitude.

The airplane is in level flight attitude when the top (directly against the side of the fuselage) of the left-hand landing gear leg is level. Use an accurate digital or bubble level placed at this location to determine when the aircraft is level. Care should always be taken when the aircraft is lifted to prevent damage to the aircraft or harm to people around it.
