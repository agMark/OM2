The hopper is made of Derakane and is heat-cured for greater chemical resistance. However, it is not good practice to leave chemicals in the hopper overnight if it is known that these chemicals have an adverse affect on fiberglass hoppers.

The hoppers attach to the fuselage frame with AN bolts and Mil Spec nuts.

Details of the upper view of the hopper tank is shown below in Fig Cross Reference. To remove the hopper tank, the following procedures may be followed:

  1. Remove the hopper gate box and gate box adapter per procedures shown under
  2. Remove the fuselage side skins on both sides of the hopper tank.
  3. Remove the upper engine cowl skins.
  4. Remove the hopper lid.
  5. If the hopper is to be removed, remove the windshield per and hopper-to-fuselage fairings.
  6. Using a chain hoist with 2 X 6 board across the bottom of the hopper throat, apply slight upward pressure to relieve load on hopper supports.
  7. Remove all bolts from the hopper supports and braces that secure the hopper tank to the fuselage structure. Movement of the chain hoist will be necessary to relieve the load on the attaching bolts.
  8. Use caution to hold the chain in the center of the opening in the top of the hopper and gently lift the hopper clear of the aircraft.

Replacement of the hopper is done by reversing the order of the above procedure. Clean all sealing surfaces and apply new sealant and gaskets.

The hoppers for the above Air Tractor models have painted-on hopper quantity markings on the outside of the hopper and the hopper wall inside the cockpit. These markings are intended to be a backup to the panel mounted hopper quantity gauge as well as a handy reference when the master switch is turned off.

If faded or incorrect markings are found, Air Tractor can supply the proper templates and marking materials to reapply the markings on the internal or external surfaces of the hopper. Service Letter #325 can provide additional information regarding the placement of the markings.
