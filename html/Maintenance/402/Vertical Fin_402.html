Details of the vertical fin are shown in Fig Cross Reference. The fully cantilevered vertical fin is attached to the fuselage frame at the front spar location with two NAS bolts and AN nuts. It has been found that these two bolts tend to loosen in service. Buffeting from the propeller slipstream during run-up and stresses from turns tend to produce constant loads from side to side that may loosen the nuts.

The rear spar attaches to the fuselage frame with AN bolts of various lengths, depending on the number of shims required between the fuselage frame and the rear spar. The shims are of various thickness (.063/.080/.125) and the upper and lower shims are different part numbers.

Fin Replacement

The vertical fin rear spar is drilled on installation where the attachment is made to the top longeron. If a new fin is installed, make a drill template from the original fin rear spar in order to drill the four 3/16" holes for the attachment to the upper longeron. The template should be 2.44" wide and long enough to pick up the two lower attach bolts. The template should be made of either aluminum or steel which is at least .063" thick.

Install the fin on the aircraft and install the bolts at the front and rear spar locations. Be sure the correct amount of shims is used between the fuselage frame and the rear spar. The rear spar bolts have a torque of 60 inch-pounds at the nut.
