The actuator assembly has a threaded collar. This collar is threaded internally to match the threads on the worm drive threads of the actuator, and this threaded externally to match the threads of the Air Tractor Tee assembly. The Tee assembly is attached to the two 1/4” aluminum arm assemblies that are bolted to the flap torque tube assembly. The Tee assembly has a steel grease shield and cap attached. The shield is attached to the T with two AN screws. The Caplug is attached to the shield with a Tyrap.

The Tee assembly that screws into the threaded collar has a AN set screw that is located on the top surface of the Tee. The threaded collar has a large diameter rounded surface, and just forward is a smaller diameter part on the collar that is to be used for tightening the collar against the Tee. Whenever it is necessary to remove the flap actuator, the factory practice is to first loosen the set screw in the T” ssembly with a small allen wrench, then place a soft rag or duct tape on the small diameter of the threaded collar (so as not to scratch the finish) and with a pair of water pump locking pliers, break loose the connection between the threaded collar and the Tee assembly. The threaded collar can then be screwed by hand from the “Tee”. Be careful not to dent or scratch the finish of the ball nut as small dents will interfere with the action of the balls inside.
