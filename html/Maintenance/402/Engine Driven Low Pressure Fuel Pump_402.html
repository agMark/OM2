Drive Pad Pattern Mismatch

Service Letter 310 described an issue that applied to serial number -0485 and prior with PT6A-67AG Build Specification (BS) 1103 engines installed where the mounting bolt pattern interfered with installation of 51076 fuel pumps. All other engines used on Air Tractor aircraft utilize studs and self locking nuts.

The PT6A-67AG BS1103 utilizes mount bolts and safety wire. This required spot faced bolt hole pattern in the fuel pump housing for clearance of the bolt head to pass the fuel pump body when mounting the fuel pump to the engine. Newly manufactured fuel pump housings with the bolt hole spacing which precisely match the engine bolt pattern will not allow for the bolt head to clear the fuel pump housing during installation.

Pratt & Whitney incorporated mounting studs and self-locking nuts into production engines beginning with serial number PCE-RD0268 introducing a new build specification (BS1314).

Replacement of the fuel pump with a newly manufactured (unmodified) fuel pump will require the, concurrent compliance of P&WC SB No. 14467 which replaces the current oil scavenge pump housing utilizing mount bolts and lock wire with one which utilizes mount studs and self-locking bolts, thereby converting to the configuration of the drive pad to match the newer build specification (BS1314).
