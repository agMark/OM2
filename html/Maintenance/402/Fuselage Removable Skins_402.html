Details of the fuselage removable skins are shown below in Fig Cross Reference.

The side skins of the fuselage are attached to the fixed skins with quarter-turn studs of various lengths. The stud has a basic part number of 1142005 and the dash number designates the length. The most common stud is 4002-3 with the next most common a 4002-4. All studs have the dash numbers on the head. Stud lengths used are -3 through -15. Stud installation requires a grommet and snap ring. Installation pliers are needed for stud installation or removal. All receptacles are p/n 1142715.

The side skins can be easily removed.

The side skins have a thin strip of neoprene to prevent chafing. This neoprene is 1/32” thick by 1” wide, comes in 10 ft. rolls and has pressure sensitive adhesive backing. A punch can be made from a piece of 3/4” OD tubing and one end sharpened on a grinder. At each quarter-turn location, the neoprene is marked and a 3/4” hole is punched.

The side skins are sheared to size, but the quarter-turn fastener holes will require drilling on installation. The old skin may be used for a pattern, or if it is damaged, ask the factory to provide a hole finder. Use a .471 diameter hole saw to drill the side skins to match the receptacles. These hole saws have a No. 30 pilot.
