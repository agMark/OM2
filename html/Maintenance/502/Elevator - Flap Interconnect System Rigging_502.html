Rigging Instructions

  1. Ensure flaps are rigged. Completely retract the flaps to the 0° position.
  2. Disconnect the elevator down spring where it connects to the aft elevator controls idler. This can be done with the flight control stick in the full forward position.
  3. After the spring is disconnected, lock the flight control stick in the neutral position using the cockpit control lock.
  4. Manually position the flaps to 10° of flap deflection. The flaps must be positioned at exactly 10°. Position the flap disconnect linkage so that the flap position indicator in the cockpit reads 10°.
  5. At the same time the flaps are at 10°, check to see if the elevator is in the neutral position. If the elevator is not in neutral at the 10° flap position, adjust the length of the interconnect linkage until the elevator is in neutral when the flaps are at 10°.
  6. Move the flaps through their complete range of deflection. Confirm that the elevator does not reach an up or down stop when the flaps are being positioned. If binding or a stop is reached, troubleshoot and correct the problem.
  7. Restore the elevator down spring to its proper position.
  8. Manually deflect the elevator full travel (up and down) and confirm that no binding or rubbing occurs at any deflection.
