The flaps are attached to the aluminum flap arms with AN bolts. The order of washers and parts with the bolts pointing inboard is a large AN washer under the bolt head (except at the boom hangar location where a regular AN washer is used), then the Fafnir bearing, steel flap arm, AN washer and AN nut.

The flap pushrod is attached to the flap with a AN bolt, large AN washer under the bolt head, steel flap arm, two AN washers and finally a AN nut as shown in Figure 2-140. The pushrod has an adjustable Fafnir bearing with an AN check nut. Be sure that the bearing is positioned to allow the pushrod to be rotated slightly from side to side.

INSERT Figure 2-140 Flap Pushrod

During 12-month inspections (See Section 3.1.15), the AN bolt through the pushrod should be checked for straightness, and if bent, both the bolt and bearing should be immediately replaced. At 100-hour intervals, remove the lower fuselage side skins under the cockpit, and while the flaps are being cycled through the full range of travel, check to see if the pushrods drag on any fuselage parts. With the flaps positioned fully down and then fully up, move the ailerons through the full range of travel and check for clearance.
