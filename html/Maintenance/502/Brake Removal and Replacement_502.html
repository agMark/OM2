Brake details are shown in Figure 2-181. For instructions concerning brake maintenance and overhaul, refer also to Cleveland (Parker) Document AWBCMM0001-22.

Brake fluid is red Mil-H-5606A petroleum-based fluid, which is commonly used in many light aircraft. Do not use automotive brake fluid as this will cause the O-rings to swell.

The wheel units can be removed by using a wrench to remove the twelve bolts attaching the two halves of each brake assembly. The brake line may be left connected if only the linings are to be charged. There are 32 linings and 64 rivets per aircraft.

Each brake assembly has four pistons, each with an O-ring.

To remove the pistons in order to change O-rings, remove the brake line and thread a 1/4-28 bolt in the piston. Pull the piston from the caliper housing. The pistons are aluminum and should be checked for wear or burrs that could cut the O-ring. Check the bore in the housing at the same time. When installing new O-rings, soak them in MIL-H-5606 brake fluid for some time before installation. Reinstall the pistons with care, ensuring that the O-ring is not damaged.
