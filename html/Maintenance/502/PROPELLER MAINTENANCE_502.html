Maintenance on your Hartzell propeller is to be done in accordance with the Propeller Owner's Manual and Logbook listed in Section 1.0.2.

Propeller pitch settings are -8.0° (reverse), 18° (low pitch) at the 30” station for the AT 502B and 504; propeller pitch settings are -11.0° (reverse), 16.5° (low pitch) at the 42” station for the AT 502A; and propeller pitch settings are -15.0° (reverse), 8.2° (low pitch) at the 42” station for the AT 502A with the PT6A-140AG engine (502XP). See the Propeller Owner's Manual for required maintenance and life limited parts.

The following references the prop governor...not a section which is included in current 500 series maintenance section. This proposed text also includes SL #360.

PROPOSED--"AT-502A aircraft fitted with the PT6A-140AG engine and four bladed Hartzell HC-B4TN-3C propellers (branded the AT-502XP) were originally approved with a Woodward P/N: A210507 Overspeed Governor installed. It has been shown that the max RPM set speed of this governor is slightly too low and may interfere with normal operation of the primary governor, causing propeller surging at takeoff power settings. Applicable aircraft serial numbers 502A-3076 and prior with PT6A-140AG engines installed (also known as “502XP” aircraft) are part of this group. Refer to Service Letter #360 to correct this concern."--PROPOSED
