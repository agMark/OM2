If any trouble develops, first check the flap circuit and flap switch circuit breakers on the panel. If this does not correct the malfunction, a visual inspection of the microswitches and microswitch arms should be made. Enter the fuselage and inspect the microswitches for broken actuator arms or loose wires. Also inspect the relays for broken or loose wires.

If the flaps are retracted but won't extend, chances are there is something wrong with the down microswitch or down relay. If the flaps are extended but won't retract, look for trouble with the up microswitch or up relay. The flaps can be manually extended or retracted by rotating the rubber coupling at the motor shaft by hand.

If the flaps extend beyond the 26° mark, check the down microswitch. Have someone cycle the flaps and see if the roller on the microswitch arm lifts properly on the striker plate and pushes the microswitch plunger in to stop the travel. If there is any doubt, push the plunger in with the flap midway through the stroke. The flap will stop if the switch is working. In this case, the down microswitch needs to be adjusted to set the travel limits.

Check the microswitches with an ohmmeter. Check continuity between the C and N.C. pins on both switches. Activate the switch by pushing the plunger in and check continuity between the C and N.O. pins. If these checks confirm continuity, the microswitches are OK and do not need to be changed. Replace any switch determined to be defective.
