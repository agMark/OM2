The 500-series Air Tractor aircraft are certificated in the FAA's RESTRICTED category, which allows the aircraft to be used for agricultural dispersal purposes. When these aircraft are operating in the RESTRICTED category, the operator may use the guidance provided in the Civil Aeronautics Manual 8 (CAM 8) and FAA Advisory Circular No. 20-33B to operate at weights higher than the certificated gross weight that is listed in the Flight Manual. This is detailed in service letter #80AA and #304. CAM 8 provides a graph detailing weight increase as it is related to load factor. The original load factor for 500 series aircraft is 3.8 which corresponds to a 31% maximum weight increase as shown in figure .

The operator may increase the maximum weight of the aircraft to the above weights after meeting the following conditions:

  1. An appropriately rated pilot performs a flight check (detailed in service letter #304).
  2. The hopper load is 4,100 lbs or less.
  3. The aircraft is never flown faster than the original maneuvering speed of 140 mph.
  4. Weather, runway length/condition, and terrain are all considered.
  5. The operator uses caution when operating the aircraft from unfamiliar runways or at elevations higher than 1,300 ft.
