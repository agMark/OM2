Details of the tail-wheel fork are shown in Fig Cross Reference. The tail wheel fork may be removed from the housing by removing the single AN stainless bolt from the arm assembly that connects to the centering springs. With the fork removed, check for wear on the spindle at the bronze bearing location. Also check for the condition of the steel bushing in which the lock pin inserts. This bushing is pressed into the steel plate at the bottom end of the spindle and staked with a center punch in four places on each side of the plate. This holds the bushing in place since it has a beveled edge on both ends. Excess wear on the fork spindle at the bronze bearing location is an indication that grease is not reaching the proper areas. To properly grease the bronze bearing, jack the tail wheel clear of the ground and apply MIL-G-81322 grease at the fitting on the housing, rotating the fork until grease is running out of the full perimeter of both upper and lower bearings. Excessive wear in the spindle will require a new fork assembly (which includes the steel lock pin bushing).
