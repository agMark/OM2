With the master switch on use of a voltmeter to check that there is power to the relays at the 88a contact. If battery voltage is measured, have someone attempt to extend and retract the flaps with the flap switch while checking for battery voltage at the relay coils. If there is no battery voltage when the flap switch is activated, check the microswitches and the flap switch(es). If there is battery voltage at the respective relay coils when the flap switch is activated, you should hear the relay “click" and the flap motor should operate. If the relay “clicks" but the flap motor does not operate, check for battery voltage at the motor. If there is no battery voltage at the motor when the relay clicks, the relay contacts are probably defective and the relay should be replaced. If the relay does not click, turn off the master switch, disconnect one of the coil wires and check the coil with an ohmmeter. The relay coil terminals are the two smaller studs located on the relay case. The resistance through the relay coil should be about 59 ohms. Use an ohmmeter to check that the relay coil is not shorted to ground. Replace the relay if the coil is defective.
