The fuel tank receiver has an adjustment screw located on the center lower side of the instrument face to adjust the “E" side of the needle range. The trimmer (adjustment) screw for the “F" side of the needle range is remotely mounted behind the upper panel and is just left of the panel centerline. Since the slotted end of the trimmer screw is oriented down, it is possible to reach under the panel with a small screwdriver to adjust the “F" trimmer screw. (Verify that the power is off).

To check the receiver settings, drain all the fuel from the aircraft and jack the tailwheel up onto a platform until the bottom of the wing tanks are level. Be sure the parking brake is ON and the main wheels are chocked as the tail is quite high in this position. An easy way to determine that the bottom of the wing tanks are level is to sight fore and aft along the tank bottom parallel with the horizon. Level the wings laterally by placing a barrel and boards under the low wing tiedown ring until both wing tips are level, but do not use the tie down rings to support the full weight of the aircraft.

Remove the left-hand fuel line from the tank and cap the left-hand tank outlet and plug the line to isolate the two tanks. Set the needle on “E" with the adjustment screw on the face of the receiver. Gradually add fuel to the right-hand wing tank while the battery switch is ON. The battery should be fully charged before attempting this check. The receiver needle should start to move slightly off the “E" mark after 2 gallons of fuel are present in the tank. To check the “F" mark, lower the tail wheel to the ground. Reconnect the plugged fuel lines and fittings. Fill both tanks completely full. Adjust the trimmer screws behind the panel until both tanks show “F".
