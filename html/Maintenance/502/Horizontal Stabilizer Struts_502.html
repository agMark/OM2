The stabilizer struts attach to the fuselage frame and stabilizer eyebolts with AN bolts. There are stainless terminals with AN check nuts at each strut end to allow for adjustment.

The struts are constructed of 4130N streamlined tubing and may be repaired if necessary. Check the factory for instructions since internal tubes are installed inside the struts. The tubes are oiled internally through the threaded fittings on the ends and should be re-oiled after repair. They are sandblasted and given a baked-on powder coating for abrasion resistance. At various intervals, the struts should be re-sandblasted and finished as called out above. Be sure to remove the terminals and plug the open holes before sandblasting.

The strut leading edges are typically protected with 3M anti-abrasion tape. Thicker protection tape or material should not be installed as damage may occur (ref SL198 and SL409). The tape should be periodically inspected for condition and replaced as required.
