The spray pump is manufactured by Agrinautics and is attached to an Agrinautics strut assembly with AN bolts.

The standard fan is a Micronair product and is intended for moderate spray volume use. Other units that are approved for use are the Arrowprop four blade wood and Weath-Aero fans. The AT-504 uses a Micronair or Weath-Aero fan.

For high volume dispersal, install an additional 40 nozzles to the spray booms and change to larger spray tips on the spray nozzles. Figure 2-34 below shows typical spray configurations. The figure also shows the orifice sizes and flow rates of the various tips at common spray pressures.

Air Tractor supplies the aircraft with Spraying Systems QU-4010 spray tips pointed 10° down from straight back. These fan tips (with a 40° fan spray angle) pointed 10° down provide excellent deposition with minimal drift.

Additional information on recommended spray pressures and droplet size is available from the factory.

The procedure for removing the spray pump follows:

  1. Remove fan pitch-control cable or brake-control cable.
  2. Loosen the hose coupling that secures the pump inlet line to the pump.
  3. Loosen the hose coupling that secures the pump outlet port to the spray valve line.
  4. Release the latches on the strut assembly and remove the pump and fan.

or

  1. Remove the strut through-bolt on the alternate configuration and remove the pump and fan.

The pump may be replaced by reversing the order of removal shown above.
