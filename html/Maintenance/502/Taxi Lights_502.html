The wiring schematic for the taxi lights is shown in Figure 2-50 below. The taxi lights (also called nose lights) use two 250-watt bulbs and are located in the extreme nose of the airplane just below the propeller hub.

The taxi light switch is located on the lower instrument panel and receives its electrical power from either the airplane bus or from the working light relay. The switch is closed to energize the coil of the taxi light, supplying power to both taxi lights.
