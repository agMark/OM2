Details of the rudder are shown in Figure 2-64. The rudder is attached to the vertical fin with three AN bolts with one AN washer under the bolt head and two under the AN nut. The rudder hinges have NMB bearings installed which are staked on each side of the 1/4" thick hinge fitting. Since there is a certain amount of drag with these spherical bearings, it is very important to fully torque (100 inch-pounds at the nut) the three AN bolts that attach the rudder to the fin. If the bolts are loose, the bolts will turn on the fin hinge brackets and cause the holes to become elongated.

The bearings exhibit little wear, but if it is necessary to replace them, construct a puller that will apply force to the outer ring of the bearing with an opposing force against the aluminum housing. Insert a AN bolt through both parts of the puller and tighten it until the bearing rim is pulled past the stake marks and the bearing comes out of the housing. Use a center punch to restake the new bearing after installation in the aluminum housing.

The rudder horn has two bushings installed for wear at the cable attachment. The horn is attached to the rudder assembly with two AN stainless steel bolts. Rudder control stops are at the rudder horn. Control limits are shown in the Type Certificate Data sheet and below for reference:

  • Left: 21° ± 1.0°
  • Right: 21° ± 1.0°

It is recommended that the cable guard be and remain installed on all aircraft. Aircraft damage due to a loose rudder cable may occur as the loss of the cable attaching hardware. Abrasion of the cotter pin from spray materials may lead to a complete loss of the attaching bolt and nut. Air Tractor has developed a stainless steel guard to the rudder cable attach hardware to prevent such a recurrence.
