The fuselage frame is made of 4130N tubing. It may be repaired in conventional ways. FAA Advisory Circular AC-43.13 may be used as a guide for repairs to tubing. All fittings are also made from 4130N material. The repaired area should be sanded and burned paint should be removed to a point where it can be feather-edged by sanding. A good etching solution is a mixture of 1/2 fluid oz. of concentrated phosphoric acid for each gallon of isopropanol (isopropyl alcohol). If relative humidity is extremely low, add 1/4 cup of distilled water to the solution. See Section 2.105.3 for complete repainting instructions.

If your aircraft does not already have an overturn skid plate installed, it is highly recommended to install one using the information located in Service Letter #97.

Reported Issues

For serial numbers 502-0037 thru 502B-0658, there have been reports of cracks or failures of the upper longeron on the left hand side of the fuselage frame just forward of the stabilizer front spar attach fitting. We believe that these cracks or failures are associated with high vertical loads during repeated hard turns. For more information regarding inspections and repairs, refer to Service Letter #195 and Service Letter #195B.
