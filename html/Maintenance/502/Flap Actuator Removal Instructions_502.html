  4. Remove the AN bolts that attach the aluminum arms on the flap tee to the torque tube.
  5. Check rubber coupling on motor shaft for excessive backlash.
With the complete actuator on the workbench, if it is desired to change the actuator itself, perform the following:

(For the Commercial Aircraft actuator only)

  1. Remove the two screws that hold the dust shield to the lower end of the assembly
  2. Use open-end wrench to remove down override stop from end of jackscrew. CAUTION: This stop uses left-hand threads.
  3. Loosen the setscrew in the top of the tee assembly with a small Allen wrench.
  4. With water pump pliers or locking pliers, break loose the ball nut from the Tee assembly.
  5. Unscrew striker by hand out of Tee assembly.

To install new actuator, use the reverse procedure, but apply a few drops of Loctite to end of jackscrew threads before attaching the left-hand threaded stop. When all parts are installed, the complete unit is ready to be installed in aircraft.
