The fixed skins are attached with machine screws and nuts. Nuts in the lower skins are stainless steel while those located in the upper fuselage areas are AN. The machine screw head style is either 100° countersunk or truss head.

The long lower skin is attached to the skin under the cockpit floor with rivets, but if it is desired to remove one skin (but not the other), the rivets can be drilled out and replaced with 90013-3 or -5 screws and stainless nuts. If the baggage floor is to be removed, it will be necessary to remove the long lower skin in order to access the nuts.

During 12-month inspections, all screws attaching the fixed skins should be checked to verify that they are tight.

The Camloc receptacles are attached to all lower skins and stringers before these parts are attached to the aircraft. If a skin or stringer is replaced, be sure to install the side skin and attach it to the part being replaced so that it is properly positioned for the side skins to fit after installation to the airframe.
