  5. Manually center the rudder and hold in place.
  6. Visually ensure that the rudder pedals are even in the cockpit. Adjust if needed.
  7. Measure the cable position to ensure that the mechanical advantage is correct. Measure from the center of the hole in the idler arm to the cable centerline (0.75 +/- 0.10 inches).
  8. If the cable position is not correct:
    1. Loosen the cable connector lock nuts.
    2. Adjust the cable length by loosening or tightening the cable adjustment sleeve (threaded section).
    3. When the correct spacing is achieved, tighten the lock nuts firmly.
    4. Ensure that the cable connector ferrule is in firm contact with the connector.
  9. Manually deflect the ailerons and rudder full travel (left and right) and confirm that no binding or rubbing occurs at any deflection. If binding is noted, troubleshoot and correct the problem.
  10. Interconnect system check:
    1. Move the ailerons full deflection left, and confirm that the rudder also moves full left.
    2. Move the ailerons to full deflection right, and confirm that the rudder also moves full right.
    3. Move the ailerons to neutral, and confirm that the rudder also moves to neutral.

Due to the numerous unsecured items in the baggage compartment, it is believed that one or more items fouled the cables causing the cables to jam resulting in a subsequent failure of the pulley.
