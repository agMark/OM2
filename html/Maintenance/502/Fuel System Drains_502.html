Refer to Figure 2-69 for the location of the fuel tank and header tank drains. These drains are designated for daily activation during the pilot's walkaround inspection as shown in the AIRPLANE FLIGHT MANUAL.

The wing tanks have Curtis drain valves and the header tank has a Curtis drain valve. If the valves leak, remove them and check the seals for nicks and look for trash between the seal and the valve seat. Apply some 3M Permatex or equivalent to the threads of all drains before they are reinstalled. Replace the valve if the seal is bad.

Air Tractor has received a report that the retaining ring at the end of the drain stem was cracked. If the retaining ring had cracked on the other side as well, the stem would have fallen out of the valve body, and all of the fuel would have been lost.

The cracked retaining ring is probably a result of allowing the stem to snap shut, with the force of the spring hammering shut the O-ring on the seat. It is suggested to ease the valve closed, rather than allowing it to snap shut. Any valve found with a cracked retainer ring must be scrapped.

The header tank drain tends to freeze up during fertilization operations if it is not activated daily. If this should happen, replace it immediately as this is the most important drain of the system. Be careful not to cross-thread drains when replacing.

PROPOSED--"For AT-502A-3103 & prior (only aircraft w/ PT6A-140AG engine); AT-502B-3050 & prior; and AT-504-4028 & prior we have become aware that the fuel drain valve on the bottom of the firewall mounted fuel filter has the potential to become damaged in flight. This damage most likely would come from a bird strike on the fuel drain valve. If an object such as a bird were to hit the drain valve in flight with enough force it could be broken or cracked and leak fuel. If a leak occurred, it is also possible that air would be sucked into the fuel system. Air sucked into the fuel system could cause a loss of engine power and the engine may flame out. To prevent an occurrence as described here, a deflector/guard for the drain valve has been designed that is fully described in Service Letter #343."--PROPOSED FOR INSERTION TO INCORPORATE SL#343.
