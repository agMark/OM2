Details of the vertical fin are shown in Figure 2-63. The fully cantilevered vertical fin is attached to the fuselage frame at the front spar location with two NAS bolts and AN nuts. It has been found that these two bolts tend to loosen in service. Buffeting from the propeller slipstream during run-up and stresses from turns tend to produce constant loads from side to side that may loosen the nuts.

Check the torque on the two NAS bolts at 300-hour intervals. The torque applied is 420 inch-pounds if the bolt is held stationary and torque is applied at the nut. The torque is 460 inch-pounds for the reverse of this procedure.

The rear spar attaches to the fuselage frame with AN bolts of various lengths, depending on the number of shims required between the fuselage frame and the rear spar. The shims are of various thickness (.063/.080/.125) and the upper and lower shims are different part numbers.

The vertical fin rear spar is drilled during installation where the attachment is made to the top longeron. If a new fin is installed, make a template from the original fin rear spar in order to locate the four 3/16" holes for assembly to the upper longeron. The template should be 2.44" wide and long enough to pick up the two lower attachment bolts. The template should be made of either aluminum or steel which is at least .063" thick.

Install the fin on the aircraft and install the bolts at the front and rear spar locations. Ensure that the correct number of shims are used between the fuselage frame and the rear spar. The NAS front spar bolts have a torque of 420 inch-pounds at the nut and the rear spar bolts have a torque of 60 inch-pounds at the nut.
