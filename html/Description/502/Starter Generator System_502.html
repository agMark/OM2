The starter-generator system produces and controls all electrical power for the aircraft. It works with the system's batteries and generator-control unit (GCU) to power the electrical buses. All electrical sub-systems receive their power from buses that are fed by the starter-generator system.

The 502/504 series aircraft are equipped with a 250-amp Lear-Siegler p/n 23048-021 28-volt starter-generator and use a p/n 23046-520 QAD mounting kit. There is an optional wet spline Lear-Siegler (Lucas) p/n 23048-028 starter-generator using the p/n 23078-010 QAD. Another optional starter-generator is the Aircraft Parts Corp. 250SG112Q which uses the 250SG1083-3 QAD. The system is derated to a 150-amp system by using a 150-amp PDLM-150 circuit breaker. The voltage regulator is a Precision Airmotive p/n 42-1001, and the starter and master relays are Cutler-Hammer p/n 6041H202A. An alternative relay is the Kissling p/n 29-314-12-903.
