The sensitive altimeter consists of an aneroid bellows that drive the two hands on the instrument face to indicate pressure altitude. The small, adjustable dial in the face of the altimeter is called the Kollsman window. This dial can be adjusted with the knurled knob on the bezel of the altimeter case to set current barometric pressure.

The altimeter is a direct-reading instrument that senses static pressure at the static pressure ports on either side of the aft fuselage of the AT-402 series aircraft.
