The Air Tractor AT-402A and AT-402B are all-metal fully cantilevered low-wing monoplanes designed especially for agricultural operations. They are powered by Pratt & Whitney Canada PT6A turboprop engines, which are highly suited to ag flying. The standard engine for the AT-402A/402B is the PT6A-15AG, rated at 680 SHP at sea level and 71 °F. An alternate engine is the PT6A-34AG, also flat rated at 680 SHP. Installation losses are very low and the full horsepower can usually be obtained at temperatures up to 71 °F for the -15AG and up to 100 °F for the -34AG engine. The engine for the AT-402A is the PT6A-11AG Build Spec (BS) 943. It is rated at 550 SHP up to 90 °F.

The propeller is a Hartzell three-blade 106" diameter constant speed prop with reversing capabilities. The hopper is made of fiberglass and has a capacity of 400 gallons (U.S.). The horizontal stabilizer is all-metal and strut braced for added rigidity. The vertical fin is fully cantilevered. The elevators and rudder are of all-metal construction and sealed to prevent chemical entry. The fuselage features removable skin panels for ease of maintenance and cleaning. High-efficiency flaps are incorporated and are interconnected with the ailerons to provide short takeoff and landing distances.
