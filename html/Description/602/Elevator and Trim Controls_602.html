The elevator and elevator trim are the primary and secondary controls for changing pitch in flight. The elevator is positioned by the control stick, moving a series of push-pull tubes to articulate the elevator. Moving the stick fore and aft will pitch the nose of the airplane down and up respectively. Unlike roll and yaw controls, elevators are not spring loaded.

Pitch trim is used to adjust steady level stick forces at specific airspeed, altitude and power settings. Elevator trim are operated by wheels on the left hand side of the pilot. Through a series of control cables, the trim wheel sets the tab to hold each control surface at a desired position.
