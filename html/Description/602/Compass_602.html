The magnetic compass is a direct-reading instrument which senses the azimuth difference in airplane heading and a bearing to magnetic north. This difference is displayed at the lubber line of the compass as the magnetic heading.

The compass is swung at the factory and a card is attached showing the compass error at cardinal headings. The compass deviation is measured with radios and lights OFF. Operation of lights, radios or other electrical equipment may cause a significant error to the compass reading.
