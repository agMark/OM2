The Lucas p/n 23048-028 250-amp, 28-volt starter-generator (SG) uses a p/n 23046-512 QAD mounting kit. The system in the AT-602 is derated to 150 amps.

The starter-generator system produces and controls all electrical power for the aircraft. It works with the system batteries and generator-control unit (GCU) to power the electrical buses. All electrical subsystems receive their power from buses that are fed by the starter-generator system.

Fig Cross Reference above shows the schematic for the 250-amp charging system. The 250-amp system is a standard part of the AT-602. The system consists of:

  1. The Starter/Generator (S/G) - the armature is continuously engaged in the engine accessory gear box. The S/G functions as an electric motor for starting the engine and then as an engine driven DC Generator.
  2. The Generator Control Unit (GCU) - The GCU is located beneath the cockpit floor on the right-hand forward side.
  3. The Line Contractor Relay (LCR) - Located on the right side of the firewall.
  4. The Start Relay - Located on the right side of the firewall.
  5. The Start Switch (ST SW) located on the lower instrument panel.
  6. The Generator Switch (GEN SW) located on the lower instrument panel.
  7. The Pilots Panel Voltmeter (VM) located on the lower instrument panel.
  8. The Low Voltage Warning Light (LV LITE) located on the upper instrument panel.
  9. 15 Amp GCU Circuit Breaker (15A C/B) Located on the right lower engine mount tube just ahead of the firewall.
