The ailerons are of all-metal construction and hinged on ball bearings. The bellcranks and pushrod ends operate on ball bearings, producing smooth operation and low system friction. The stick forces are light and the ailerons are very responsive. The light stick forces are a result of the installation of aerodynamic servo tabs installed on the outboard end of each aileron and a leading edge cuff.

The ailerons are interconnected with the wing flaps so that the ailerons droop 10° while the flaps move down to 30°. The motion of the interconnect is non-linear so that most of the aileron droop occurs during the first half of the flap travel. This provides optimum conditions for takeoff performance.

Each aileron has a large balance weight on the outboard end, just forward of the leading edge. These weights must never be removed as they are necessary for flutter prevention.
